🔒 GDPR Ground Rules
The most important 5 minutes of the workshop. These rules protect you, your service users, and your employer.
⚠️
This is not about restricting you. It is about making sure you can use AI confidently without putting yourself at risk. Learn the boundaries once — then work freely within them.
⚖️ The Core Ruling — Understand This First
Under UK GDPR, a pseudonym does NOT remove the legal risk. Even if you rename John as Sam, the combination of age + diagnosis + behaviours + incidents makes an individual identifiable.
❌ NEVER — Even with a Fake Name ✅ ALWAYS SAFE
Type a real service user's name into any public AI tool Build a Fictional Support Persona with characteristics only — no real incidents
Describe a real incident with real dates, locations, or details Upload your policy documents — these are organisational, not personal data
Feed real behavioural patterns of a specific person into AI Use AI to practise writing with fictional scenarios only
Use 'Sam instead of John' — the GDPR risk remains Write the real report from memory in your employer's secure system
📋 The 5 Rules — Say These Out Loud
Read each rule and tick it once you have understood it
Rule 1: Never type the real name of a service user into any public AI tool. Ever. Use a fictional name for practice only.
Rule 2: Never describe a real incident with real details in an AI tool. Practise with fictional scenarios only.
Rule 3: Use AI to learn how to write reports. Write the actual report from memory in your employer's secure system.
Rule 4: NotebookLM is safe for your policy documents — these are organisational documents, not personal data.
Rule 5: If you are ever unsure whether something is safe to share with an AI tool — assume it is not. Check with your manager first.
🔄 The Safe Workflow — Memorise This
AI is your training ground — not your filing cabinet
1
Practise with AI using fictional scenarios
Build your fictional persona. Use it for all report writing practice. No real names, no real incidents.
2
Build the skill
Learn the structure, the language, the format. Get comfortable with how professional reports read.
3
Apply it mentally in real situations
After your real shift, mentally apply what you have practised to the real events you witnessed.
4
Write the real report in your employer's secure system
Type it yourself, in your organisation's system, using the structure and language AI helped you learn.
💡
The quality of your reports improves. The speed increases. Your confidence grows. And your service users stay protected. Everyone wins.

🤖 Claude AI — Report Writing
The 3-part prompt formula. The fictional persona. The master report prompt. Everything you need to write professional reports in under 5 minutes.
🧩 The 3-Part Prompt Formula
Every powerful AI prompt has the same three ingredients
1
The ROLE — who should AI act as?
"You are a professional care documentation assistant..." — AI instantly adjusts its tone, depth, and knowledge based on the role you assign.
2
The CONTEXT — everything AI needs to know
"I am a support worker. Here is Alex's profile. Here is what happened during my shift..." — the more context you give, the better the output.
3
The OUTPUT — exactly what you want produced
"Format with these sections: Overview, Activities, Mood, Concerns, Actions, Next shift..." — if you don't specify the format, AI will guess.

❓ How to Use This Library
What is a prompt exactly? ▼
A prompt is the instruction you give to an AI tool. The better your instruction, the better the output. Think of it like briefing a very capable assistant — the more context and detail you give, the more useful their response will be.
Do I need to use these prompts word-for-word? ▼
No — they are starting points. Over time you will adapt them to your own style and the specific people you support. The key parts to keep are the Role (who AI acts as) and the Output (what format you want). The context section is where you personalise.
Can I save these prompts somewhere? ▼
Yes — the easiest way is to create a note on your phone called "AI Prompts" and paste each one you use regularly. In Claude, you can also create a Project and pin your most-used prompts. Manuel will cover this in AI Leverager.
What if the AI gives me a wrong or strange answer? ▼
Always review what AI produces before using it. AI can make mistakes — especially with specific policies or procedures it does not have in its context. For NotebookLM, check the citation. For Claude, edit the output to match the real situation before submitting any report.
